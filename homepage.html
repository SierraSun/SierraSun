Pediatric asthma more likely near Logan Airport

Does living close to the airport increase the risk of childhood asthama? According to logan airport health study final report, children who live in neighborhoods bordering Logan International Airport are as much as four times more likely to wheeze, experience shortness of breath, and exhibit other signs of undiagnosed asthma compared with children who live farther away.

From the chart above, we can tell that Boston area have much higher rate of hospital admission for asthma. Therefore, how about the condition in East Boston, which near Logan airport?

Air pollutants –– the main trigger of pediatric asthma

The Massachusetts state had conducted a comprehensive environmental health assessment of Logan Airport. In the report, it examined the prevalence of respiratory outcomes among children for those living in three levels airport-related air pollution exposure areas.
High exposure: Living in the areas where have high exposure to air pollutants due to the airport.
Medium exposure: Living in the areas where have medium exposure to air pollutants due to the airport.
Low exposure: Living in the areas where have low exposure to air pollutants due to the airport.

The main air pollutants are comprise of PM2.5 and NOx. Area near Logan airport has more concentration of NOx and PM2.5. Which means, you will have more exposure to the air Pollutants when you live near Logan Airport. You can see the heatmap bellow:

Based on study, high relation between the concentration of air pollutants or the exposure degree, and the posibility to get asthma has been found.

Children will have more probability to have asthma when living in HIGH EXPOSURE area

As you can see from the chart, probable asthma was more prevalent in the high exposure area than in the medium or low exposure areas. Similarly, the prevalence of chronic bronchitis increased as the potential for exposure increased. However, current asthma among children were similar across all three exposure areas.
