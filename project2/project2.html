One Case

Police Arrest Man in Connection with Armed Robbery

On Monday, Jan. 8 at 6:30 a.m. a mother and her son were held at knife point and robbed. The suspect then indecently exposed himself to the victims in the 200 block of Saratoga Street.

Hearing a commotion in the hallway, a neighbor in one of the home’s other apartments ran downstairs and yelled at the suspect. Simons, District 7 Community Officer, did not release the suspects name due to some further evidence gathering as part of the ongoing investigation but said police are confident they have their man.

“We believe based on the description of the suspect by the three victims, the type of car used in both attacks and the M.O. (modus operandi) used in both attacks the man arrested was the suspect we were looking for,” said Simons.

How Do?

people living in East Boston feel about

Emily G.

I lived in East Boston for two years and loved it. Affordable, abundant street parking, good food (mostly hole-in-the-wall Latin American spots), short commute to downtown on the arguably the most reliable train line.

Dave F.

I lived in East Boston for five years, and pretty much hated it. I was, as Emily warned against, living north of Central Square, which sucked. I tried biking when I worked in Medford, and biking through the Chelsea/Revere area is a horrible experience that will threaten to shake the fillings out of your teeth.

Julia F.

My family is from East Boston, some of them still live in Day Sq in the house they grew up in. I think it's a nice place to live, especially since they did all the work on the parks, ecc- especially Jeffries point.
